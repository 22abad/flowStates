# Cognitive Economics

## 1. Quantifiable Progress

Today, I executed the Lab Exam in approximately 17 minutes.
This metric stands in stark contrast to my previous performance of 61 minutes. The outcome is deterministic.
This data point validates a key hypothesis: The past two months of intensive LeetCode practice did not merely accrue knowledge; it fundamentally upgraded my **Coding Proficiency**. Standard academic requirements have been rendered trivial.
This is a strong validation of the rigorous methodology. The ROI is tangible.

## 2. Resource Allocation Failure

However, the system has a flaw.
I realized I have been operating in a "Single-Threaded" mode. My cognitive resources were 100% allocated to technical ingestion during transit.
The result? A resource allocation failure: I abandoned my backpack on the train.
Upon arrival at the library, I noted a strange "lightness." The bag was missing.
It contained personal assets and electronics.
Notably, my reaction was not panic, but a rational audit: _"Primary assets (MacBook, iPad) are secure."_
I lost a container, but retained focus. My working memory was so saturated with algorithmic logic that the background process of "asset retention" was terminated.

## 3. System Upgrade: From Sequential to Concurrent

Life, unlike a discrete algorithm, requires concurrency.
I cannot sustain an 18-hour coding sprint indefinitely. There are exams, family logistics, and operational overhead.
The exam today was a proof of concept for **Burst Capacity**. The requirement now shifts to **Stability**.
The new challenge is not solving "Hard" algorithmic problems, but solving the problem of **Context Switching**.
I must upgrade my operating system from sequential processing (batch mode) to concurrent processing (multitasking).
The objective is to maintain intensity while restoring situational awareness.

# 认知经济学

## 1. 可量化的进步

今天，我在大约 17 分钟内执行了实验考试。
这一指标与我之前 61 分钟的表现形成鲜明对比。结果是确定性的。
这个数据点验证了一个关键假设：过去两个月高强度的 LeetCode 练习不仅仅是积累知识；它根本性地升级了我的 **代码掌控力**。标准的学术要求变得微不足道。
这是对严格方法论的有力验证。投资回报率是实实在在的。

## 2. 资源分配失败

然而，系统存在缺陷。
我意识到我一直以“单线程”模式运行。我的认知资源 100% 分配给了通勤途中的技术摄入。
结果呢？资源分配失败：我把背包落在了火车上。
到达图书馆后，我注意到一种奇怪的“轻松感”。包不见了。
里面有个人资产和电子配件。
值得注意的是，我的反应不是恐慌，而是理性的审计：_“主要资产（MacBook, iPad）是安全的。”_
我丢失了一个容器，但保持了专注。我的工作记忆被算法逻辑填得太满，以至于“资产保留”这个后台进程被终止了。

## 3. 系统升级：从顺序到并发

生活不同于离散算法，它需要并发。
我无法无限期地维持 18 小时的代码冲刺。还有考试、家庭后勤和运营开销。
今天的考试是 **爆发力** 的概念验证。现在的需求转向 **稳定性**。
新的挑战不是解决“Hard”级别的算法问题，而是解决 **上下文切换** 的问题。
我必须将我的操作系统从顺序处理（批处理模式）升级到并发处理（多任务）。
目标是在保持强度的同时恢复情境意识。

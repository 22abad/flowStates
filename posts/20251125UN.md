# Focus in Chaos

## 1. The Reality of Shared Living

I have returned to my budget accommodation—a high-density shared rental in Dublin.
The demographic is diverse, primarily consisting of gig economy workers and construction staff. The atmosphere is one of raw survival.
The kitchen presents a logistical challenge. Resources like milk and fresh produce often disappear due to the lack of clear boundaries. Even laundry is subject to the unpredictability of the shared environment.
It is not necessarily malicious; it is simply the dynamic of a resource-constrained environment.

[END]

[ZH]

# 混乱中的专注

## 1. 合租生活的现实

我回到了我的廉价住所——都柏林的一处高密度合租房。
这里的人口结构多样，主要由零工经济工作者和建筑工人组成。气氛中弥漫着原始的生存气息。
厨房是一个后勤挑战。由于缺乏明确的界限，牛奶和新鲜农产品等资源经常消失。甚至洗衣也受制于共享环境的不可预测性。
这不一定是恶意的；这仅仅是资源受限环境的动态。
[END]

[EN]

## 2. Unpredictable Disturbances

Last week, I was awakened at 2:30 AM.
The disturbance was a heated altercation between roommates, involving fluent, intense language.
It was a stark reminder of the volatility of my living situation. The conflict resulted in a change of room assignments, and I now share my space with a new, diverse group of roommates.
My room has become a microcosm of international cohabitation:

- Top bunk: A French resident.
- New roommate: An Egyptian resident involved in the recent conflict.
- Myself: Focused on coding in the corner.

[END]

[ZH]

## 2. 不可预测的干扰

上周，我在凌晨 2:30 被吵醒。
骚乱源于室友之间激烈的争吵，伴随着流利、激烈的语言。
这赤裸裸地提醒着我居住环境的不稳定性。冲突导致了房间分配的变动，我现在与一群新的、背景各异的室友共处一室。
我的房间变成了国际同居的缩影：

- 上铺：一位法国住户。
- 新室友：一位卷入近期冲突的埃及住户。
- 我自己：专注于角落里的编码。
  [END]

[EN]

## 3. Internal Order

It sounds chaotic. It _is_ chaotic. Yet, I have found a paradoxical peace here.
While external conflicts over territory or resources occur, I remain immersed in my work. The noise becomes background static; the chaos accentuates my internal order.

This afternoon, I felt frustration at a near-miss in class.
But tonight, amidst this disordered environment, I achieved a state of high productivity. Implementing Monotonic Queues, DFS, and BFS algorithms became a rhythmic, almost meditative process.
I proved a critical point to myself: **External chaos cannot impede internal logic.**
Let the environment be noisy. The code remains silent, logical, and structured.

[END]

[ZH]

## 3. 内部秩序

听起来很混乱。确实很混乱。然而，我在这里找到了一种悖论般的平静。
当关于地盘或资源的外部冲突发生时，我依然沉浸在我的工作中。噪音变成了背景静电；混乱反而突显了我内部的秩序。

今天下午，我因课上的一次失误而感到沮丧。
但今晚，在这个无序的环境中，我达到了高产出的状态。实现单调队列、DFS 和 BFS 算法变成了一个有节奏的、近乎冥想的过程。
我向自己证明了一个关键点：**外部混乱无法阻碍内部逻辑。**
让环境嘈杂去吧。代码依然是安静的、逻辑严密的、结构化的。
[END]
